About Me



I’m not your typical entry-level candidate.

Over 12 years ago, I graduated from a technical high school with a focus on networking and telecommunication. Since then, I’ve been continually developing my passion for technology and cybersecurity. Although my career path initially took me in a different direction, I always stayed engaged with technology, learning and growing my skill set.

After facing some early challenges — including limited resources during my school years and financial hardships — I made a decisive commitment to pursue cybersecurity. I reorganized my life, refocused my efforts, and set myself on a clear path to success.

While working 50–60 hours a week, I dedicated my evenings and weekends to studying cybersecurity. I obtained my CompTIA Security+ certification and began gaining hands-on experience through labs, incident response simulations, and threat detection projects. I’ve honed my skills in security monitoring, analysis, and response.

What sets me apart isn’t speed — it’s consistency. I am disciplined, committed, and focused on long-term growth. In the past, I transformed my physical health through sports and became a certified personal trainer. Now, I’m applying the same approach to my cybersecurity career, building my knowledge and skills step by step.

My goal is clear: to secure my first role in cybersecurity, specifically within a Security Operations Center (SOC), where I can apply my skills to monitor, detect, and respond to security incidents. I am fully dedicated to immersing myself in the field and growing as a professional. While I am still growing in the field, I’m someone who will earn your trust and contribute meaningfully over time.

If you’re looking for someone who is driven, dedicated, and ready to make an impact in your team — feel free to contact me.
